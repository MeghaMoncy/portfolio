Projects
Educational EHR Deployment

Collaborating within a dynamic team, I played a pivotal role in the successful implementation and improvement of an educational Open-source Electronic Health Record system- OpenEMR. The project involved deploying OpenEMR instances on local machines and integrating real clinical data from MIMIC-IV to create a robust educational platform. The system was seamlessly integrated into 6 graduate and 3 undergraduate health informatics courses, covering competencies such as Clinical Information Systems and Health Information Management. Beyond educational aspects, we led a project focused on the secure deployment of the EHR system, adhering to stringent data protection standards to comply with healthcare regulations. This included establishing a secure phpMyAdmin Docker setup with SSL-secured Apache reverse proxy and implementing robust cybersecurity measures, including system monitoring and alerts.

Cybersecurity Enhancement in Healthcare

Conducting an independent study on Cybersecurity Enhancement in Healthcare, I showcased a commitment to proactive and multi-faceted cybersecurity strategies. Leveraging Python and MS Excel, I investigated the critical role of staff training and advanced technical solutions in mitigating cyber threats. The study culminated in the orchestration of a proactive cybersecurity solution specifically tailored for Emergency Departments. This involved deploying Email Filtering Systems and Incident Response Plans, providing a comprehensive defense mechanism against ransomware threats. My work showcased a keen understanding of the evolving landscape and the need for robust defenses against cyber threats in the healthcare sector.

Vulnerability Management in Practice

As a Security Analyst Intern in the NSF Funded ACCESS Student Training and Engagement Program (STEP), I actively contributed to the enhancement of cybersecurity through practical and proactive vulnerability management strategies.Demonstrating a commitment to innovation, I employed strategic host scans and script automation to identify and address vulnerabilities promptly. In addition to hands-on security measures, I developed a comprehensive transition management spreadsheet that integrated data from Qualys, Syslog, and Nagios. Additionally, I optimized business unit roles, minimizing the need for assigning Global Admin status and streamlining access management within the ACCESS project. I compiled my findings and contributions into a comprehensive paper, offering a detailed overview of vulnerability trends, mitigation strategies, and the impact of streamlined processes on cybersecurity efficiency.

Analysis of Breach Notification Portal

I conducted a comprehensive mixed-methods study to dissect 4,751 breach notification reports from the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. Leveraging Python for data processing, Atlas.ti for qualitative discourse analysis, and Tableau for visualization, I conducted both quantitative and qualitative analyses to provide a holistic understanding of healthcare security breaches. Quantitative analysis played a pivotal role in my study, where I employed statistical methods to examine yearly trends, identify frequently involved entities, and analyze the frequency of different breach types. Complementing the quantitative findings, I employed qualitative discourse analysis, incorporating thematic coding of textual descriptions. My findings and analyses culminated in a presentation and poster titled "Sociotechnical Aspects of Healthcare Security Breaches," presented at WISP 2023.

Biomedical Analytics in Public Health

My academic coursework in Advanced Biomedical Analytics in Public Health involved a comprehensive exploration of health data analysis using DHIS2 and R, showcasing my proficiency in leveraging advanced tools and techniques to derive actionable insights. Utilizing DHIS2 and R, I conducted extensive health data analysis, revealing critical insights into various health indicators. In addition to traditional analysis, I applied advanced forecasting techniques and trend analysis to predict future health outcomes. The incorporation of drill-down maps allowed for a more granular examination of data, facilitating the identification of nuanced patterns that informed strategic planning in public health analytics.To enhance data visualization and communication, I designed interactive DHIS2 dashboards featuring dynamic scatter plots and choropleth maps.
